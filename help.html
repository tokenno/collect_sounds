Sound Hunt - Help

Getting Started

Welcome to Sound Hunt! This app lets you explore your surroundings to find hidden sounds using GPS and audio cues. Follow these steps to get started:

  • Start Tracking: Click the "Start Tracking" button to enable GPS tracking and audio playback. This will also resume the audio context on mobile devices.
  • Select a Target: Choose a target (1 to 4) from the "Select Target" section to focus on finding a specific sound.
  • Enable Compass: Use the "Enable Compass" button to display a compass that points toward your selected target.
  • Move Around: Walk around to get closer to the target. The app will play beeps that increase in frequency as you approach the target (within 30 meters). When you're within 5 meters, the sound will play continuously, indicating you've found it.
  • Adjust Volume: Use the Mixer sliders to adjust the volume of each sound (1 to 4).
  • Sound Toggle: Use the "Sound Off"/"Sound On" button to mute or unmute all sounds.
  • Reset All: Click "Reset All" to reset the app to its initial state, clearing all progress, recordings, and settings.

Manual Mode

Manual Mode allows you to set your own target points instead of using pre-set coordinates.

  • Enable Manual Mode: Click "Toggle Manual Mode" to enable it. The button will change to "Disable & Reset".
  • Set GPS Points: For each target (1 to 4), click the "Set GPS" button to set the target's location to your current GPS position.
  • Disable Manual Mode: Click "Disable & Reset" to return to using pre-set coordinates (if available). This will reset all manually set points.

Recording Sounds in Manual Mode

In Manual Mode, you can record your own sounds for each target and associate them with the GPS points you set. These recordings can be played back when you approach the target, just like the default sounds.

  • Enable Manual Mode: Click "Toggle Manual Mode" to enable Manual Mode.
  • Set a GPS Point: For a target (e.g., Manual 1), click "Set GPS" to set its location to your current position.
  • Record a Sound:
    • After setting the GPS point, a "Record Sound" button will appear next to the target’s coordinates.
    • Click "Record Sound" to start recording. The button will change to "Stop Recording".
    • Speak, make a sound, or capture ambient noise for a few seconds, then click "Stop Recording".
    • The recording will be saved, and the app will play it back once immediately to confirm.
  • Playback:
    • The recorded sound will play when you approach the target (within 30 meters), just like the default sounds.
    • If a recording exists for a target, it takes priority over the default sound for that target.
  • Delete a Recording:
    • After recording, a "Delete Recording" button will appear next to the target in Manual Mode.
    • Click "Delete Recording" to remove the recording. The app will revert to using the default sound (if available) for that target.
  • Storage: Recordings are saved in the browser’s IndexedDB storage, so they persist even if you close and reopen the app, until you delete them or clear your browser data.

Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, try the following:

  • GPS Not Working: Ensure location services are enabled on your device and that the app has permission to access your location.
  • No Audio: Click "Start Tracking" to enable audio playback. Ensure your device is not muted and the "Sound Off" button is not active.
  • Recording Fails: Ensure the app has microphone permissions. On mobile devices, serve the app over HTTPS to enable microphone access.
  • Compass Not Showing: Select a target and ensure the target has a set location (either pre-set or manually set in Manual Mode).
